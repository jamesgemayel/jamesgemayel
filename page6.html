DESIGN II

This course focused on the relationship between human movement and architectural form. I mapped the movement of a man on crutches, tracking key points such as the hands, legs, knees, and head. These mapped points were then used to generate three-dimensional shapes, each following a single movement trajectory. The individual forms were later reconfigured into a cohesive pavilion, translating dynamic motion into spatial design.
